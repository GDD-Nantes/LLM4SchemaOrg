#  Your ideal trip to England

The dozen best places and areas that should be on you list when visiting
England. Choose you own order but visit as many as you can.

  * [ London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London):  While it's not impossible to plan a trip to England without visiting London, it's certainly not to be advised, as the nation's sprawling capital boasts plenty of attractions to keep you busy.
  * [ Edinburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edinburgh):  One of Scotland's most attractive cities, the capital city of Edinburgh is also one of England's most visited destinations.
  * [ Bath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bath,_Somerset):  Although one of England's smaller cities, Bath more than makes up for its diminutive size with a multitude of things to see and do.
  * [ Stonehenge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stonehenge):  One of the planet's oldest World Heritage Sites, Stonehenge has been a place of pilgrimage for more than 4,500 years.
  * [ Windsor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire):  The historic town of Windsor, conveniently located a short train ride west of London, offers plenty of fun things for tourists to do.
  * [ The Cotswolds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotswolds):  Covering almost 1,287 square kilometers of pristine countryside, the beautiful Cotswolds is undoubtedly one of the most photographed corners of England.
  * [ The Lake District](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_District_National_Park):  In the north west of England and covering an area of 1,448 square kilometers is more of England's most beautiful scenery: the Lake District National Park.
  * [ York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/York):  One of northern England's most popular tourist destinations, the medieval city of York, long the ecclesiastical capital of the Church of England, boasts one of the country's most magnificent cathedrals.
  * [ Oxford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford):  England has long been a center of learning, with Oxford being one of the two most famous university towns also ranking highly as tourist destinations.
  * [ Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridge):  England has long been a center of learning, with Cambridge being one of the two most famous university towns also ranking highly as tourist destinations.
  * [ Canterbury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canterbury):  Pay a visit to historic Canterbury, and you'll soon discover why this beautiful city continues to be such a draw for visitors to England.
  * [ York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loch_Ness):  Despite the fact that the legends of mythical monsters have largely been debunked (just don't tell the locals), spectacular Loch Ness remains an extremely popular tourist attraction for travelers heading to Scotland."
